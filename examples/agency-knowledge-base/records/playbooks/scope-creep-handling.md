# Handling scope creep

When a client request adds work beyond the SOW, the playbook is:

## Detect

The agent watches Linear and Notion for new requests on active
projects. Anything that adds > 3 hours of work or extends the
timeline triggers an alert in the per-client internal Slack channel.

## Surface (not silently absorb)

Within 24 hours of detection, the PM:
1. Quantifies the scope delta (hours, timeline impact, deliverable changes).
2. Posts a clear note to the client channel: *"This request is
   outside the current SOW. Here's what it adds: X hours, Y days
   to launch. Want us to scope a change order, or de-prioritize it?"*
3. Never starts the work until the client confirms which path.

## Resolve

- **Change order:** scope the addition, get client signoff in
  HelloSign, update Notion + Harvest project codes.
- **De-prioritize:** acknowledge, log to backlog, set expectation
  it won't ship this phase.
- **Absorb (rare):** only with Dana's signoff. Used when the
  request is small + the client relationship needs an investment.

## Why this works

Clients respect the explicit conversation. The agency loses money
on absorbed scope creep; clients lose trust on surprises mid-project.
Surfacing within 24h, with quantification, makes scope a
transparent negotiation rather than a hidden cost.
