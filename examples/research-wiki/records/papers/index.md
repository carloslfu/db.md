# records/papers

- [[records/papers/silver-alphazero-2017]] — DeepMind's AlphaZero: one self-play + MCTS + neural-net recipe reaching superhuman chess, shogi, and Go with no domain knowledge beyond the rules  ·  #reinforcement-learning #self-play #mcts #deepmind
- [[records/papers/tesauro-tdgammon-1995]] — Tesauro's TD-Gammon: a neural-network backgammon player trained by self-play with TD learning that reached world-class strength — the first famous self-play success  ·  #reinforcement-learning #self-play #temporal-difference #value-network
- [[records/papers/silver-alphago-2016]] — Silver et al.'s AlphaGo: the first program to beat a Go professional, combining supervised + RL policy networks, a value network, and MCTS — predecessor to AlphaZero  ·  #reinforcement-learning #self-play #mcts #go #deepmind
- [[records/papers/schulman-ppo-2017]] — Schulman et al.'s PPO: a first-order policy-gradient method whose clipped surrogate objective keeps updates near the old policy without TRPO's constrained solve  ·  #reinforcement-learning #policy-gradient #trust-region #ppo
- [[records/papers/schulman-trpo-2015]] — Schulman et al.'s TRPO: a policy-gradient method that bounds each update by a KL-divergence trust region, guaranteeing monotonic improvement on a surrogate objective  ·  #reinforcement-learning #policy-gradient #trust-region #trpo
- [[records/papers/mnih-a3c-2016]] — Mnih et al.'s A3C: asynchronous advantage actor-critic, where parallel workers explore independent environment copies, decorrelating updates without a replay buffer  ·  #reinforcement-learning #deep-rl #actor-critic #a3c #deepmind
- [[records/papers/mnih-dqn-2015]] — Mnih et al.'s DQN: a deep Q-network learning Atari from pixels at human level, stabilized by experience replay and a periodically-frozen target network  ·  #reinforcement-learning #deep-rl #dqn #atari #deepmind
- [[records/papers/williams-reinforce-1992]] — Williams' REINFORCE: the class of likelihood-ratio policy-gradient estimators that optimize a parameterized policy directly by following the gradient of expected reward  ·  #reinforcement-learning #policy-gradient #reinforce #foundations
- [[records/papers/watkins-qlearning-1992]] — Watkins & Dayan's Q-learning convergence proof: an off-policy TD control rule that learns the optimal action-value function regardless of the policy followed  ·  #reinforcement-learning #q-learning #off-policy #temporal-difference
- [[records/papers/sutton-td-1988]] — Sutton's foundational temporal-difference paper: TD methods learn predictions from successive estimates (bootstrapping) rather than waiting for the final outcome  ·  #reinforcement-learning #temporal-difference #prediction #foundations
