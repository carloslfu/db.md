# AI as co-thinker

The pattern of treating an LLM as a thinking partner you invite in
from the start of a task, rather than a tool you reach for after
you're already stuck.

## Key sources

- Mollick, *Co-Intelligence* (2024) — "always invite AI to the
  table." See [[sources/reading/2026-05-15-mollick-co-intelligence]].
- Karpathy, *Software 3.0 talk* (June 2025) — frames the LLM as the
  partial-autonomy partner ranging from autocomplete to fully
  agentic.

## My open questions

- Does this work for purely creative tasks (drafting fiction,
  composing music) the same way it works for analytical ones?
- Where does "co-thinker" end and "co-author" begin? Attribution?
- How do I keep my own taste calibrated when I always have a
  collaborator?

## Practice

I default to opening the chat alongside any thinking task that
isn't strictly mechanical. The cost of inviting is zero; the
ceiling is high.
