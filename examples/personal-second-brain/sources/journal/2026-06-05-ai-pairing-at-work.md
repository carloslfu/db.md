# 2026-06-05

Good work day. Spent the morning designing the data-migration plan with
the LLM open the whole time, not as a search engine I tab over to when
I'm stuck but as the thing I think out loud at from the first blank page.
Mollick's "invite it to the table" is just true now — by the time I'd
have normally opened it (stuck, frustrated) I was already three good
iterations in.

What worked: I set the autonomy dial low on the parts I have taste about
(the rollback strategy, the cutover ordering) and high on the parts I
don't care to hand-do (generating the test fixtures, drafting the runbook
boilerplate). Karpathy's slider, in practice. The trick is knowing which
parts are which, and that's the judgment the tool can't have for me.

The open worry, still: when it's always there, am I outsourcing the
struggle that used to build my own intuition? I caught myself accepting a
schema suggestion I didn't fully follow, then made myself re-derive it.
That re-derivation is the tax I have to keep paying or the skill atrophies.

Texted Sam about it tonight — they're feeling the same thing on the
writing side, that the draft comes easier but they have to fight to keep
their own voice in it. Same tension, different craft.
