# 2026-06-14

22 miles in the Marin Headlands this morning, the biggest day I've put
together for the fall 50k. Sam came along for the first half before
peeling off at Tennessee Valley. Cold fog the whole climb up from Rodeo
Beach, then it burned off at the top and we got the ridgeline to
ourselves.

The thing I'm proud of: I went out slow on purpose. Every other long run
this spring I've torched the first hour and crawled home. Today I sat on
my hands for the first 90 minutes — ate a gel before I was hungry, kept
the climbs to a hike — and the back half was the strongest I've felt all
year. The Jurek lesson finally landed in my legs and not just my head.
"Go out slow" reads like a platitude until the day it saves your race.

Sam and I talked the whole first half about their writing project — they
hit 40k words and they're in the part where it stops being fun and
starts being work. I told them about attention residue and time-blocking
and they immediately wanted the book. Funny how the running buddy and the
maker friend are the same person.

Legs are wrecked in the good way. Two more long ones and a taper and I
think the 50k is real.
