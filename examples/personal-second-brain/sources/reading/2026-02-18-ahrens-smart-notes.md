# Highlights — Ahrens, "How to Take Smart Notes", ch. 3

> Fleeting notes are reminders of what's in your head. Literature
> notes capture what you don't want to forget from what you read.
> Permanent notes are written for your future self and stored in the
> slip-box, linked to other notes — not by topic, but by context of use.

> The trick is to write each permanent note in full sentences, in your
> own words, as if for publication. If you can't write it clearly, you
> haven't understood it yet.

> The slip-box isn't an archive; it's a communication partner. You ask
> it questions by following links, and the surprising connections it
> surfaces are the raw material for new writing.

My note: this is the book that finally made my note system click. The
"context of use, not topic" rule for links is the bit I kept getting
wrong — I was filing by subject like a library, not threading by idea.
Rebuilt my linking habit around it. Feeds
