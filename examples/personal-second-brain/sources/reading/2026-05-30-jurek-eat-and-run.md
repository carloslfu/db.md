# Highlights — Jurek, "Eat and Run", ch. 5

> "Sometimes you just do things." The mantra he repeats when the
> rational case for stopping is overwhelming and he keeps moving anyway.

> On ultras, the race is mostly a fueling and patience problem. Go out
> slow, eat before you're hungry, drink before you're thirsty, and the
> back half takes care of itself.

> Running long is less about the legs than about staying present —
> narrowing the world to the next switchback, the next aid station,
> the next ten steps.

My note: I came for the training detail and stayed for the patience
argument. "Go out slow" is the lesson I keep relearning — every time I
blow up on a long run it's because I ran the first hour like it was the
only one. Feeds [[records/themes/trail-running]], and it directly
shaped how I ran the [[sources/journal/2026-06-14-marin-headlands-long-run]].
