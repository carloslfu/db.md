# Highlights — Newport, "Deep Work", ch. 1

> Deep work: professional activities performed in a state of
> distraction-free concentration that push your cognitive
> capabilities to their limit. Shallow work: logistical-style tasks,
> often performed while distracted, that don't create much new value.

> "Attention residue" — when you switch from task A to task B, a
> residue of A stays stuck to your attention and degrades B. The
> switch itself is the tax, not the new task.

> Treat depth like a real appointment. If it isn't on the calendar
> as a block you defend, the shallow work will expand to fill the day.

My note: the attention-residue idea reframes my context-switching
problem. It's not that any one interruption is expensive — it's that
the residue lingers long after. I started time-blocking a 90-minute
deep block before email. Feeds [[records/themes/deep-work]], and the
practice connects to my note-taking system in
[[records/themes/tools-for-thought]].
