# Deep work

Attention treated as the scarce resource it actually is — protecting
long, distraction-free blocks for the work that pushes my limits, and
recognizing that the cost of switching is the residue, not the
interruption itself.

## What the theme is

Newport's split: **deep work** (focused, limit-pushing, value-creating)
versus **shallow work** (logistical, distracted, low-value). The
practical core for me is two ideas:

- **Attention residue.** Switching from A to B leaves a residue of A
  stuck to my focus that degrades B. The switch is the tax. This is why
  a "quick" Slack check costs more than the 30 seconds it takes.
- **Schedule depth like a meeting.** If a deep block isn't on the
  calendar as something I defend, shallow work expands to fill the day.

## My practice

A 90-minute deep block first thing, before email or Slack. It holds
maybe three days a week, which is more than I managed before I named
the residue.
