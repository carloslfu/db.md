# Trail running

Building a real trail-running practice, currently pointed at a fall
50k. This is a life theme, not a work one — the garden is for the whole
of me, and the trails are where I do the thinking I can't do at a desk.

## What I'm learning

- **Patience beats speed.** The recurring lesson from every long run:
  go out slow. I blow up exactly when I run the first hour like it's the
  only one. Jurek's "go out slow" reads like a platitude until the day
  it saves the run — which it finally did on 06-14.
- **Fueling is a discipline, not an instinct.** Eat before hungry,
  drink before thirsty. On long efforts the race is a fueling-and-
  patience problem more than a fitness one.
- **Moving meditation.** Narrowing the world to the next switchback is
  the same attention move I'm chasing in [[records/themes/deep-work]],
  just with legs.

## My open questions

- How much weekly volume do I actually need for a 50k without breaking
  myself? I keep guessing.
- Is the "moving meditation" real, or am I just too tired to ruminate?
  Either way it works.

## The plan

Two more long runs and a taper. Most of these miles I run with
[[records/people/sam-reyes]], who's training alongside me — half the
practice is the company.
