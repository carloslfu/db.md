# Engage Gunderson for the Halcyon DPA review

Acme engaged outside counsel [[records/companies/gunderson]] to redraft
the [[records/companies/halcyon]] data-processing addendum, rather than
redline it in-house, because Halcyon's compliance team requires updated
EU sub-processor language and a 72-hour breach-notification window
before they will countersign the renewal.

[[records/contacts/priya-nair]] made the call on the security review
[[records/meetings/2026-05-22-halcyon-security-review]]. From the
transcript [[sources/transcripts/2026/05/2026-05-22-halcyon-security-review]]:

> **Priya Nair:** That's outside what I'll redline myself — I'm going to
> bring in our outside counsel, Gunderson, to turn the DPA around
> properly. I'd rather get it right than have your team bounce it.

She confirmed the engagement and budget to [[records/contacts/tom-becker]]
the same afternoon in [[sources/emails/2026-05-22-priya-tom-gunderson]]
(expect a ~USD 3,500 invoice, coded to legal).

Alternatives considered: redline in-house (rejected — risk of a second
bounce from Halcyon's compliance team), ask Halcyon to draft it
(rejected — slower, and their template was the problem), or accept the
existing addendum (rejected — it predates the EU sub-processor changes).
The invoice lands as [[records/expenses/2026-05-21-gunderson]].
